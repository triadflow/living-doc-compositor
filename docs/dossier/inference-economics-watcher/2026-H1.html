Eight points of capability, 250× the cost.

The 2026-H1 inference-economics frontier does not show a single market steadily getting cheaper. It shows two markets, separated by roughly two orders of magnitude on price for an eight-point gap on capability.

Rene Luijk · Apr 21, 2026 · 7 min read · economics inference cost monitoring
TL;DR

Closed-frontier models (Opus 4.7, GPT-5, Gemini 3 Pro) sit between roughly $5 and $75 per million output tokens with GPQA Diamond scores between 80 and 87. Open-weight near-frontier (DeepSeek V3.2, Llama 4 Maverick, Gemini 3 Flash) sits between 30¢ and $1 per million output tokens with GPQA between 70 and 75.

That is a 250× cost ratio for an 8-to-12-point capability gap. The useful question for most builders in 2026 is no longer which model runs best. It is whether the last eight points of capability are worth paying two hundred and fifty times as much to cross.

The 2026-H1 inference-economics story is usually told as price decline — every frontier lab priced below the 2024 GPT-4o band. Correct on average, wrong in particular. The April 2026 snapshot of the frontier does not show a single market steadily getting cheaper. It shows two, separated by two orders of magnitude for an eight-to-twelve-point gap on GPQA Diamond.1Note 1Methodology in force for 2026-H1: capability measured on GPQA Diamond + SWE-bench Verified + AIME 2025; cost on list $/M input, list $/M output, cached $/M input; latency on TTFT at 1k-context plus sustained throughput. April 2026 baselines from public pricing pages and artificialanalysis.ai / Scale SEAL. A saturation-rotation policy is written into the underlying doc. On one side: DeepSeek V3.2, Llama 4 Maverick, Gemini 3 Flash, priced between roughly thirty cents and one dollar per million output tokens, with GPQA between seventy and seventy-five percent. On the other: Claude Opus 4.7 at seventy-five dollars per million output tokens, with GPQA at roughly eighty-seven.2Note 2DeepSeek V3.2 is the cost-leader at roughly $0.30 per million output on Together / Fireworks. Opus 4.7 list is $75 per million output. The ratio is 250× — for a capability gap of 8 to 12 GPQA points, not 80. The useful question in 2026 is no longer which model is "best." It is whether the gap between frontier and near-frontier is worth paying two hundred and fifty times as much to cross.

The frontier, flattened to two axes

Put cost on a log axis and capability on a linear axis, and the cost-side pareto frontier — the line connecting the cheapest model at each capability tier — runs almost entirely through open-weight territory. DeepSeek V3.2 holds the low-cost corner. Gemini 3 Flash holds the mid-tier corner on closed weights, backed by TPU economics GPU-based labs cannot cleanly match.3Note 3Gemini 3 Flash at ~$0.60 per million output is Google pricing against the Groq / Together / Fireworks tier rather than against the closed-frontier tier. TPU cost-per-inference is the structural backdrop — GPU-based providers at comparable throughput and margin would be loss-making. Llama 4 Maverick sits at or near DeepSeek's point. Google's Gemma 4, released April 2 and covered separately below, sits further up the capability axis at similar pricing. No US closed-weight first-party frontier model appears on the pareto line below roughly eighty-two GPQA.

Second, the frontier line has a visible knee. Capability improves gradually through the $1 to $20 per million output band — GPT-5, Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3 Pro cluster there with GPQA between eighty and eighty-five. Then a jump to eighty-seven at Opus 4.7, a roughly seven-fold price step for those last two-to-three points. For workloads that price correctness absolutely, that seven-fold is worth it. For the other ninety percent, it is a rounding error on capability and a multiplier on bill.

Cache discounts are where the real cost is

List prices are a headline number no serious builder pays. Three of four frontier labs offer cache discounts of seventy to ninety percent on repeated input, and Anthropic raised its Opus and Sonnet cache discount to ninety percent in March.4Note 4Anthropic's March 2026 cache-discount move: cached input billed at 10% of list, up from a prior 25%. Makes Opus economics viable for agentic loops where the same system prompt is reused hundreds of times per task — a cached Claude Code session can cut effective total cost by 60–80%. For agentic workloads that reuse the same system prompt, Opus 4.7's effective cost becomes seventy-five dollars output on a one-dollar-fifty cached-input stream — shifting the economics from premium-only to mid-tier-accessible for the exact workload pattern Claude Code and its peers drive.

As cache-discount adoption approaches universal through 2026-H2, the effective cost for cached agent workloads will diverge sharply from headline list price. The frontier and open-weight markets will not converge, but the practical gap for the cached-agent case closes — from two hundred and fifty times down to roughly fifty. Still substantial; no longer civilization-scale.

Saturation is the real clock

Benchmarks saturate. GPQA Diamond, the reference discrimination benchmark since late 2024, is now at eighty-seven at frontier — the score that read as "the edge of conceivable" eighteen months ago.5Note 5GPQA Diamond is graduate-level science Q&A with expert-curated answers. Top score drift: ~55% in late 2024, ~72% mid-2025, ~87% early 2026. Saturation zone begins around 95% where the benchmark stops discriminating. Rotation candidates for 2026-Q3: Humanity's Last Exam (frontier ~25–30%) and FrontierMath (frontier ~15%). In the saturation zone, the benchmark no longer discriminates — it becomes a floor. Within twelve months, the frontier discussion shifts to Humanity's Last Exam and FrontierMath, where frontier scores sit in the twenties and teens.

What this means for the chart: the capability axis rotates. The eight-point gap flattens as ceilings compress; new benchmarks reshuffle rank ordering. The cost axis, by contrast, stays readable — dollars per million tokens does not saturate. That is why the monitor runs on explicit benchmark rotation, not a fixed "intelligence per token" ratio. Benchmarks are a lens; lenses change.

Speed is a separate market

One axis the cost-capability chart does not show is latency. Custom-silicon providers — Groq's LPUs, Cerebras, SambaNova — serve open-weight models at an order of magnitude above GPU-based throughput.6Note 6Groq on Llama 4 Maverick at around 2,000 output tokens per second in March 2026. GPU-based frontier providers sit between 80 and 200 tokens per second. For a 10,000-token response, the difference is seconds versus a full minute — a categorical change in what a real-time agent feels like. Closed-weight labs are responding with speculative-decoding and warmed-endpoint tiers — narrowing the gap without closing it. Workloads that price latency above incremental capability have already migrated to open-weight plus custom-silicon. The correctness chart and a latency chart, not drawn here, overlap only partially.

Adoption is migrating faster than revenue

The cost gap raises the question: if the ratio is 250×, why has most workload not moved? The tiers are not interchangeable even where the chart puts them close. Closed-frontier captures revenue — legal drafting, production code review, anything where correctness is priced absolutely, which is where the reported ARR concentration sits.9Note 9Press-attributed, Q1 2026: Anthropic ~$4B+ ARR range, OpenAI ~$12B+ ARR through late 2025. Neither audited. Revenue concentration sits on the top tier (Opus / GPT-5) while token volume shifts downward — the revenue-volume divergence is the empirical signature of the price-pressure thesis. Open-weight captures volume — developer experimentation, agentic loops, emerging-market traffic, the long tail migrating to DeepSeek, Llama, and Gemini 3 Flash faster than revenue has caught up. If volume leads revenue, the pressure is real but delayed. March 2026's cache-discount aggression is the first pre-response — the 250× compressing from the top, because the alternative is losing the volume margin that subsidises the next training run.

Continued decline expected if DeepSeek-style open-weight pressure persists and Gemini 3 Flash holds its aggressive Flash-tier pricing.7Note 7From the underlying Inference Economics Watcher doc's indicator-frontier-cost-median card, 2026-H1 baseline. The forecast is conditional on two things staying true: the Chinese open-weight cadence holds its current release tempo, and Google does not retreat from Flash-tier pricing as TPU capacity tightens. Both are non-trivial conditions.

A late-period entrant that could redraw the chart

Eighteen days before this period closed, Google shipped Gemma 4 — a four-size open-weight family under Apache 2.0. The 31B variant reports 84.3% on GPQA Diamond and 89.2% on AIME 2026, hosted at roughly thirty-eight cents per million output tokens on day one. If the claims hold under independent adversarial evaluation — and that verification has not yet arrived — Gemma 4 31B sits three GPQA points below Opus 4.7 at two hundred times lower cost, collapsing the eight-to-twelve-point gap this chart turns on. The adoption signal over the first seven days is the strongest corroborating evidence: ten million Gemma 4 base-model downloads, over a thousand community derivatives, and national-government language programs already training on top.10Note 10Omar Sanseviero (Google DeepMind) at the AI Engineer conference, April 2026: 10M Gemma 4 base-model downloads in 7 days, 500M+ cumulative Gemma family lifetime, 100K+ community models across the family. National-government programs: AI Singapore (Southeast Asian languages) and Sarvam (Indian government-backed). E-variants use a novel Per-Layer Embeddings (PLE) architecture that loads 2B params to GPU while the rest lives in CPU/disk — enabling on-device inference at phone / Raspberry Pi scale. Google is now the only lab running closed-frontier and open-weight tiers simultaneously. The thesis stays as stated; it is flagged for revision at the 2026-Q3 check-in if the numbers survive third-party testing.

Eight points of capability. Two hundred and fifty times the cost. For most workloads, the question has flipped.

What resolves through 2026-H2

Several threads resolve before the next print. Chief among them: whether Gemma 4's reported GPQA number holds under independent evaluation. Secondary: whether AIME 2025 saturates and triggers a rotation;8Note 8AIME 2025 at frontier is already above 90%. Saturation typically arrives between 95% and 98% depending on test-case noise. Rotation candidates: USAMO 2026, FrontierMath, or curated subsets of competition math pools. Methodology change is itself a decision-record entry in the underlying doc. whether the next Llama / DeepSeek / Qwen closes the open-vs-closed gap; whether speculative-decoding closes the Groq throughput lead; and whether cache-discount adoption goes universal.
